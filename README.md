# No More Home Mental Load System


A practical guide to taking your household out of your head and into AI.


This system was built out of personal necessity — not as a concept, but as something I actually use. It combines AI assistants, automation tools, and calendar sync to make the invisible work of running a home visible, shared, and manageable.


---


## Why this exists


Women do most of the world's unpaid domestic and care work. In many households, even equality-minded ones, one person still holds most of the **mental load** — the invisible, constant work of remembering, planning, tracking, and anticipating what needs to happen.


After reading *Fair Play* by Eve Rodsky, I implemented many of its ideas. Even with good agreements in place, I was still the one holding the whole system in my head. One morning I decided to move that system into AI.


This is the exact road I followed: every step, every prompt, every tool.


---


## What's in this system


### Step 1 — Brain dump everything
Make the invisible visible. Map every recurring task, who does it, and who *remembers* it. These are often two different people.


### Step 2 — Give unpaid work a real number
Estimate hours for both execution and mental load. Connect it to money using minimum wage as a reference. See the full picture.


### Step 3 — Build your household assistant
Turn your family context into a system that generates weekly plans, grocery trackers, pet reminders, birthday alerts, and message drafts — triggered by a single command.


### Step 4 — Add cycle tracking (optional)
Integrate body awareness to adapt energy and support. Includes phase-based exercise suggestions and weekly partner messages.


### Step 5 — Connect to Google Calendar
Export the full plan to a shared calendar with emojis, recurring events, and alerts for the entire year.


### Step 6 — Automate your messages
Connect n8n + WhatsApp to send reminders automatically. No more chasing tasks or sending the same coordination messages every week.
